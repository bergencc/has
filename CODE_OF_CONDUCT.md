# Code of Conduct

## Our community
Hide and Seek is a student-centered project. We welcome participants of all backgrounds, identities, and experience levels. We value curiosity, kindness, and collaborative learning.

## Our standards
We expect all contributors and community members to:
- Be respectful, inclusive, and considerate in words and actions.
- Assume positive intent, ask clarifying questions, and give constructive feedback.
- Share knowledge and mentor others, especially newer students.
- Respect privacy and personal boundaries.
- Follow campus policies and applicable laws.

## Unacceptable behavior
The following are not tolerated:
- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind.
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Doxxing, stalking, or sharing personal information without consent.
- Threats of violence, intimidation, or encouraging unsafe actions.
- Disruptive behavior that prevents others from participating.

## Student safety and wellbeing
Because this project supports campus events, safety comes first:
- Do not design or encourage activities that could endanger participants.
- Do not collect or expose sensitive student data.
- Avoid features that enable real-time location tracking or coercion.
- Follow accessibility best practices to ensure all students can participate.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately to the maintainers.

Reports will be handled with care and discretion. Retaliation for reporting is not tolerated.

## Enforcement
Maintainers will review and investigate all reports. Actions may include:
- A private warning
- Temporary or permanent removal from project spaces
- Reversion of changes
- Escalation to institutional channels when appropriate

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces (repositories, issues, discussions, chats, events) and to community interactions related to this project.

## Acknowledgment
This policy is informed by widely used community standards and adapted for a student-centered environment.
